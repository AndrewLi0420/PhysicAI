# Design: PhysicAI — AI-Powered Sports Injury Assessment & Recovery

## Problem Statement

Athletes with acute sports injuries face a broken triage pipeline: see a sports doctor (weeks wait), get a referral, schedule PT (more weeks), pay hundreds of dollars — all before getting a recovery plan. The current workaround is a fragmented mess: YouTube, Reddit, asking a coach, or just doing nothing. There is no fast, personalized, accessible path from "I just rolled my ankle" to "here's your day-by-day recovery protocol."

PhysicAI cuts this down to minutes: an adaptive AI questionnaire diagnoses the likely injury, assesses severity, and generates a personalized recovery plan — the way a physical therapist would, without the pipeline.

## Demand Evidence

- Founder personally went through the full pipeline (sports doctor → referral → PT scheduling → cost) and experienced the friction firsthand.
- Multiple athletes in the founder's network confirmed the same problem independently.
- Current workarounds: YouTube self-diagnosis, asking coaches/trainers, paying out of pocket to skip the referral queue, or simply not treating the injury. All of these are inadequate — and the existence of all four workarounds confirms the problem is real and unaddressed by any single solution.

## Status Quo

Athletes with acute sports injuries currently:
1. YouTube/Reddit self-diagnose (free but generic, often wrong, no personalization)
2. Ask a coach, athletic trainer, or athletic friend (informal, inconsistent)
3. Pay $200+ out of pocket for a direct sports med or PT visit (expensive, friction)
4. Do nothing and rest (common — the pipeline is slow enough that they give up)

Cost: Days to weeks of delayed recovery, risk of re-injury, lost playing time.

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

**Primary user:** College and high school athletes who play team sports (basketball, soccer, football, lacrosse, etc.) — have urgency (playing time at stake), no team athletic trainer or limited access to one, and are tech-savvy enough to use a chat-based app.

**Why this wedge:** The founder has direct distribution access to this population (plays/coaches in this world). These users have the highest urgency (season stakes, not just personal fitness), the lowest access to professional care, and the highest willingness to try an alternative.

**MVP scope:** Full acute-to-recovery arc — intake assessment, severity classification, day-by-day recovery protocol, and daily check-ins through return to sport. The acute triage moment (first 24-48 hours) is the entry point that drives discovery; the daily check-in loop is what creates retention and validates the product's actual value.

## Constraints

- FDA/regulatory: Must be framed as "recovery guidance" and "educational information" — not clinical diagnosis. Every output includes a red-flag escalation ("seek medical care if X") and a disclaimer. This is how WebMD, symptom checkers, and current telehealth apps navigate this.
- Distribution: B2C first. College/HS athletes find and use it themselves. No selling to schools or athletic departments for MVP.
- Competition: YouTube + Reddit + doing nothing — NOT Sword Health or Hinge Health (who target chronic MSK conditions via employer/insurer B2B contracts, a completely different market).

## Premises

1. Target user = college/HS athletes with acute sports injuries needing same-day guidance.
2. Product = "recovery guidance" tool, not a medical device. Regulatory positioning is critical.
3. B2C model — athletes discover and use it directly. No B2B distribution for MVP.
4. Real competition = YouTube, Reddit, doing nothing — not the enterprise MSK incumbents.
5. MVP = acute triage entry point + full recovery arc. Both ship together.

## Landscape Context

**EUREKA:** Sword Health and Hinge Health absorbed ~$2B and dominate chronic MSK (back pain, joint rehab) via B2B (employer/insurer contracts). The acute sports injury triage segment — especially for college/HS athletes — is entirely orphaned. These users have no employer to sell through, no insurance that moves fast enough, and no athletic trainer on staff. The incumbents' B2B model structurally prevents them from serving this user. That's the gap.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Adaptive Questionnaire MVP
Single-session questionnaire → recovery plan. No return behavior, no ongoing tracking. Fastest to build but doesn't validate the retention loop.

### Approach B: Full AI PT Assessment (Chosen)
Multi-turn adaptive conversation → severity assessment → personalized day-by-day recovery protocol with daily check-ins and progressive exercise unlocks. Covers acute triage AND early recovery tracking.

### Approach C: Zero-Friction Bot
WhatsApp/web chat link, no account required. Maximum distribution, minimum product. Good for early validation experiments but not a durable product.

## Recommended Approach

**Approach B: Full AI PT Assessment**

**Three-screen core flow:**
1. **Injury Intake** — Body map + quick-tap chip grid to select injury location. Low friction, accommodates different interaction styles.
2. **Adaptive Assessment** — Multi-turn conversational AI (Claude API). Tap-to-answer cards (not typing) to minimize friction. AI adapts question sequence based on prior answers (e.g., "pop" on ankle → ligament flag → different downstream questions). Progress indicator (Question 3 of ~8) reduces abandon rate.
3. **Recovery Plan** — Structured output: severity classification (Grade I/II/III), recovery timeline estimate, phased recovery plan (RICE → ROM → Return to Sport), today's exercises, red-flag escalation criteria, daily check-in CTA.

**Daily check-in loop:** Every phase transition requires a check-in (not just the last one). Phase N+1 is locked until the athlete completes a check-in at the end of Phase N. Check-in data model: pain level (1-10), swelling (Y/N), weight-bearing (Y/N). Decision logic:
- pain ≤ 3 + weight-bearing = Y → advance to next phase
- pain 4-6 or no improvement → hold: repeat current phase exercises for another day, re-check tomorrow
- pain ≥ 7 or swelling worsening → escalate to red-flag screen, prompt to seek care
This loop creates the daily return behavior that transforms a one-session tool into a recovery companion.

**Assessment branching:** Rule-driven decision tree (not LLM free-form) for the question sequence. Claude is NOT used during the assessment phase — it is called exactly once, after the decision tree completes, to generate the recovery plan from structured inputs (injury type, severity grade, user profile). This ensures consistency and debuggability for a safety-sensitive assessment.

The decision tree spec must be produced as a separate artifact before implementation begins. Stub example for ankle:
```
START: injury location = ankle
  → "How did it happen?" [twist/roll | direct impact | overuse | unknown]
  → twist/roll: "Did you hear or feel a pop?" [yes | no | unsure]
    → yes: SET ligament_flag=true → "Can you bear weight?" [yes | no]
      → no: SET grade=III, seek_care=true → END TREE (escalate, no plan)
      → yes: SET grade=II → continue to swelling/bruising questions
    → no: SET grade=I → continue to function questions
  → direct impact: route to fracture screening branch
  → overuse: route to stress injury branch
```
Full tree for all 9 supported injury types must be authored before implementation.

**Exercise content:** MVP uses a curated library of ~60-80 validated exercises, authored with reference to standard PT protocols and reviewed by a licensed PT before launch. Claude selects exercise IDs from this library (by exercise_id) when generating the plan — it does not free-form generate exercise descriptions. This eliminates the re-injury risk from hallucinated guidance. Prompt-engineering-only exercise generation is NOT acceptable for MVP.

**Red-flag escalation criteria (require PT approval before launch):**
- Unable to bear any weight
- Visible deformity or suspected fracture
- Neurovascular symptoms (numbness, tingling, loss of pulse distal to injury)
- Severe swelling within 2 hours of injury
- No improvement after 72 hours
- Any head/neck/spine injury mechanism

**Structured output schema (sample JSON):**
```json
{
  "injury_type": "Lateral Ankle Sprain",
  "severity": "Grade II",
  "timeline_days": [10, 21],
  "phases": [
    { "label": "Days 1-3", "name": "RICE + Pain Control", "description": "..." },
    { "label": "Days 4-10", "name": "Range of Motion + Strength", "description": "..." },
    { "label": "Days 11-21", "name": "Balance + Return to Sport", "description": "..." }
  ],
  "todays_exercises": [
    { "exercise_id": "ankle-alphabet", "sets": 3, "reps": null, "duration_sec": 60 }
  ],
  "red_flags": ["Unable to bear weight", "Severe swelling within 2 hours"],
  "seek_care": true  // set by decision tree when Grade III or red-flag branch reached;
                     // when true: UI shows escalation screen with red-flag messaging,
                     // no recovery plan is generated or shown
}
```

**Technical stack:**
- Claude API (claude-sonnet-4-6) for final plan generation only. Rule-driven decision tree handles assessment branching. Model fixed at Sonnet for MVP — re-evaluate post-launch based on user feedback.
- Mobile-first web app (no app store required for MVP)
- Supabase for user accounts, assessment history, check-in data
- **Notifications: Resend (or equivalent) triggered via Supabase Edge Functions on a per-user schedule.** Email is primary for MVP. Web Push API is a v2 enhancement — iOS Web Push requires Home Screen install and iOS 16.4+, making it unreliable for the iPhone-dominant college athlete demographic. Risk: email open rates for college athletes may be low — monitor Day 2 return rate; add SMS (Twilio) if 40% target is not being hit.

**Onboarding / auth:** Guest mode for first assessment (no account required). Prompt to save plan at the Recovery Plan screen. Account creation = magic link to email. No password.

**PT review gate:** The first 10 AI-generated plans are held in a review queue (not delivered to users immediately). A licensed PT reviews each plan within 24 hours and approves or flags it. Only after PT approval is the plan delivered to the user. After 10 approved plans with no material changes, the hold is lifted and plans deliver immediately. If the PT flags a plan, the specific decision tree branch is corrected before any more plans are generated from that path.

**Wireframe:** Three-screen flow sketched during design session (injury intake → adaptive assessment → recovery plan with locked phases). Wireframe file is ephemeral — commit a screenshot to the repo before starting implementation.

## Open Questions

1. **Monetization:** Free with ads? Freemium (3 free assessments, then $9.99/mo)? School/team license later? Defer until 50 users.
2. **Liability/disclaimer:** Legal review of disclaimer language before launch. "Not a substitute for medical advice" is table stakes — but the language matters.

**MVP injury scope (9 types — concussion excluded):**
ankle sprain, knee sprain, hamstring strain, groin strain, shoulder sprain/impingement, lower back strain, shin splints, wrist sprain, finger sprain. Concussion is deferred to v2 — it requires a separate clinical protocol, different legal review, and a no-plan escalation-only flow that adds complexity without adding product value at MVP scale.

## Success Criteria

- **Week 1:** 10 real college/HS athletes complete the full assessment flow
- **Week 2:** 40%+ return the next day to check in and do Day 2 exercises
- **Month 1:** 50 total users, qualitative feedback from 5+ that the recovery plan was helpful
- **Month 3:** At least 3 users report returning to sport faster than expected (measured via a 30-day follow-up text/email survey sent automatically at plan completion)

## Dependencies

- Claude API access (anthropic.com)
- Curated exercise library (~60-80 exercises, schema: `{exercise_id, name, description, sets, reps/duration, phase}`) sourced from NATA/NSCA protocols
- Licensed PT to: (1) review exercise library + red-flag escalation criteria before any user sees the product; (2) review the full decision tree stub for all 9 injury types before implementation; (3) operate the 24hr review queue for the first 10 AI-generated plans post-launch (gate described in Recommended Approach above).
- Decision tree spec artifact (full branching logic for all 9 injury types) — must exist before engineering starts.

## Reviewer Concerns

Issues that persisted through 3 rounds of adversarial review — resolve before engineering starts:

1. **Decision tree runtime representation unspecified.** The doc describes the tree conceptually and with one ankle stub but does not define the data structure (JSON config, state machine, hardcoded branching?), how state is stored client vs. server, or the handoff format to Claude. An engineer cannot implement without this. Recommended: JSON config with `{question_id, options, next_node_id, set_flags}` nodes; evaluated client-side; collected flags passed as structured context to Claude for plan generation.

2. **PT review queue operational gap.** The 24hr hold is specified but the review interface, PT identity, fallback if review exceeds 24hrs, and what happens when a plan is flagged are not defined. Resolve: identify the PT, create a minimal admin view (even a simple email digest), and define the founder-escalates fallback.

3. **Guest session lifecycle undefined.** Guest mode allows assessment without an account, but the fate of guest data (plan accessible via URL? session token? discarded after N hours?) is unspecified. Define before implementation — this affects Supabase schema design.

4. **Data retention / HIPAA adjacency.** Health-adjacent data (injury type, severity, daily pain levels) requires a stated data handling policy before launch. Confirm HIPAA does not apply (no covered entity relationship), define retention period, and state whether data is identifiable.

# Visual Design System

*Added 2026-03-25 by /design-consultation*

## Aesthetic Direction

- **Direction:** Performance-Grounded
- **Decoration level:** Minimal — typography and whitespace do the work
- **Mood:** Warm authority. The app feels like a trusted expert you can access at 11pm after rolling your ankle. Confident, calm, capable. Not clinical-cold (Kaia Health), not green-wellness-corporate (Hinge Health).
- **Core principle:** Users are athletes in a temporary setback — not patients with chronic pain. The design should feel like a performance optimization system, not a symptom tracker. Type-forward — the assessment questions and recovery data ARE the interface.
- **Competitive gap:** Hinge Health owns warm-green. Kaia Health owns electric navy. No product in the space uses warm earth tones. The burnt sienna accent is completely unoccupied territory.

## Typography

- **Display/Questions:** Cabinet Grotesk 800 — confident, slightly editorial, strong at mobile sizes. This is PhysicAI's typographic voice.
- **Body/Options:** DM Sans 400/500/600 — humanist, highly readable at 14–16px on mobile.
- **UI Labels/Metadata:** DM Sans 700, uppercase, letter-spacing 0.12–0.14em
- **Data/Numbers:** Geist 500/600, `font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums`
- **Code:** Geist Mono

## Spacing

- **Base unit:** 4px
- **Screen padding:** 20–24px horizontal
- **Card padding:** 16px (answer cards) / 20px (content cards)
- **Card gap:** 8–10px between list items
- **Section gap:** 28–32px between sections

## Layout

- **Max content width:** `max-w-sm` (384px) centered — all screens
- **Border radius hierarchy:**
  - Buttons: 10px
  - Cards/inputs: 12px
  - Large content cards: 14–16px
  - Pills/badges: 999px
- **Rule:** Never uniform — size correlates with element scale

## Motion

- **Easing:** `ease-out` entering · `ease-in` exiting · `ease-in-out` moving
- **Duration:** micro 80–120ms · short 150–200ms · medium 250–350ms
- **Assessment question transition:** slide-in-from-right, 200ms ease-out (keep existing)
- **`prefers-reduced-motion`:** All transitions disabled
